Biscuit Method

Quick-Breads

Similar to the Pastry-blend method. Flour and all dry ingredients are combined. Fat is then "cut into" the flour mixture until it resembles coarse cornmeal. Liquid is added last. Dough is mixed just until moistened. Sometimes a short knead is done(CraftyBaking, 2013)

Creaming Method

Butter Cookies

It is done with sugar beaten into stick butter, margarine or shortening (solid, plastic fats), until light and fluffy. Eggs are then added. Finally, flour and dry ingredients added.

Measuring

Jug Measure

Dry ingredients

Liquid ingredients should be measured in jugs. Set the jug on a flat surface and check at eye level.
